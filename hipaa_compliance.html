Executive Summary

SDMMJTracker is fully compliant with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996 and its subsequent amendments. Our platform handles Protected Health Information (PHI) for South Dakota medical cannabis patients in strict accordance with federal healthcare privacy regulations.

This documentation outlines our comprehensive approach to HIPAA compliance, including administrative, physical, and technical safeguards implemented to protect patient privacy and ensure secure handling of sensitive medical information.

Patient Rights Under HIPAA

Right to Access

Patients have the right to inspect and obtain copies of their PHI.

  • • Request processing within 30 days
  • • Electronic copies when possible
  • • Reasonable copying fees may apply
  • • Direct third-party transmission available

Right to Amendment

Patients may request corrections to their PHI if they believe it is inaccurate.

  • • Written request required
  • • Response within 60 days
  • • Justification for denials provided
  • • Appeal process available

Right to Restrictions

Patients may request limitations on how their PHI is used or disclosed.

  • • Treatment, payment, operations restrictions
  • • Individual recipient limitations
  • • Platform feature opt-outs
  • • Marketing communication controls

Right to Confidential Communications

Patients may request communications through alternative means or locations.

  • • Alternative contact methods
  • • Secure messaging preferences
  • • Mailing address options
  • • Communication timing controls

Breach Notification Procedures

Breach Definition

A breach is the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under HIPAA that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

Notification Timeline

1

Immediate Discovery (0-24 hours)

Incident containment and preliminary assessment

2

Risk Assessment (24-72 hours)

Determine if breach notification is required

3

Patient Notification (Within 60 days)

Direct notification to affected individuals

4

HHS Notification (Within 60 days)

Report to Department of Health and Human Services

5

Media Notification (If required)

Public notice for breaches affecting 500+ individuals
