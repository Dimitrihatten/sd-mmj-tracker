Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to digital accessibility

Effective Date: January 1, 2024

Last Updated: January 1, 2024

SD MMJ Tracker is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

Our Commitment

We believe that everyone should have equal access to information and functionality on our website. We are committed to providing an inclusive experience for all users, including those with disabilities.

This commitment aligns with our HIPAA compliance goals and our mission to serve all medical cannabis patients in South Dakota.

Accessibility Standards

Our website strives to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

We have implemented features to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards, including:

  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Screen reader compatibility
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios
  • Resizable text up to 200%
  • Alternative text for images
  • Proper heading structure
  • Focus indicators
  • Accessible forms with proper labels

Accessibility Features

Keyboard Navigation

  • All interactive elements are accessible via keyboard
  • Tab order follows logical page structure
  • Skip links allow jumping to main content
  • Escape key closes modal dialogs and menus
  • Arrow keys navigate through menu items

Screen Reader Support

  • Semantic HTML markup
  • Proper ARIA labels and descriptions
  • Live regions for dynamic content updates
  • Descriptive link text
  • Form labels associated with inputs

Visual Accessibility

  • High contrast color schemes
  • Scalable text and interface elements
  • Clear focus indicators
  • No reliance on color alone for information
  • Sufficient spacing between interactive elements

Motor Accessibility

  • Large touch targets (minimum 44x44 pixels)
  • No timing requirements for essential functions
  • Ability to pause, stop, or hide animations
  • Multiple ways to interact with content

Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

Screen Readers

  • NVDA (Windows)
  • JAWS (Windows)
  • VoiceOver (macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack (Android)
  • Narrator (Windows)

Browser Compatibility

  • Chrome (latest version)
  • Firefox (latest version)
  • Safari (latest version)
  • Edge (latest version)

Medical Information Accessibility

We pay special attention to the accessibility of medical information:

Patient Data

  • All patient information is clearly labeled
  • Medical terms include plain language explanations
  • Important alerts are announced to screen readers
  • Data tables include proper headers and captions

Form Accessibility

  • Clear instructions for all form fields
  • Error messages are descriptive and helpful
  • Required fields are clearly marked
  • Progress indicators for multi-step processes

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, we acknowledge some current limitations:

  • Some third-party content may not meet our accessibility standards
  • Complex data visualizations may have limited screen reader support
  • PDF documents are being updated to meet accessibility standards

We are actively working to address these limitations in future updates.

User Preferences

Our website respects user preferences and system settings:

Motion and Animation

Users who prefer reduced motion will see minimal animations and transitions.

Color and Contrast

Users with high contrast preferences will see enhanced color schemes.

Font Size

Text can be enlarged up to 200% without loss of functionality.

Testing and Evaluation

We regularly test our website's accessibility through:

  • Automated accessibility testing tools
  • Manual testing with assistive technologies
  • User testing with people with disabilities
  • Regular audits by accessibility professionals
  • Ongoing monitoring of user feedback

Continuous Improvement

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We are committed to:

  • Regular accessibility audits and testing
  • Staff training on accessibility best practices
  • Incorporating accessibility into our development process
  • Staying current with accessibility standards and guidelines
  • Responding promptly to accessibility feedback
