HIPAA Compliance Policy

Notice of Privacy Practices

This Notice of Privacy Practices describes how SDMMJTracker may use and disclose your Protected Health Information (PHI) and your rights regarding your health information under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996.

Our Commitment:

We are committed to protecting your medical information and maintaining the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information in accordance with HIPAA regulations.

Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI includes any individually identifiable health information that we collect, maintain, or transmit. This includes:

  • Medical cannabis card numbers and verification status
  • Allotment usage and purchase history
  • Date of birth and verification information
  • Medical conditions and physician recommendations
  • Contact information and demographic data
  • Payment and insurance information

Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Treatment:

We may use or disclose your PHI to facilitate your medical cannabis treatment, including coordination with healthcare providers and licensed dispensaries.

Payment:

We may use or disclose your PHI to obtain payment for services provided, including billing and collection activities.

Healthcare Operations:

We may use or disclose your PHI for healthcare operations, including quality assessment, compliance monitoring, and business management.

Required by Law:

  • State reporting requirements for medical cannabis programs
  • Public health and safety investigations
  • Court orders and legal proceedings
  • Law enforcement activities as permitted by law

Your Rights Under HIPAA

  • Right to Access: You have the right to inspect and copy your PHI
  • Right to Amend: You may request amendments to your PHI if you believe it is incorrect
  • Right to Restrict: You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI
  • Right to Alternative Communication: You may request communication through alternative means
  • Right to an Accounting: You may request an accounting of disclosures of your PHI
  • Right to Complain: You may file complaints regarding our privacy practices

Security Safeguards

Administrative

HIPAA training, access controls, incident response procedures

Physical

Secure data centers, workstation security, device controls

Technical

Encryption, access logs, authentication systems

Privacy Policy

Information We Collect

Personal Information:

  • Name, date of birth, and contact information
  • Medical cannabis card number and expiration date
  • Medical conditions and physician recommendations
  • Purchase history and allotment usage
  • Payment information and billing details

Technical Information:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Usage patterns and interaction data
  • Cookies and tracking technologies

How We Use Your Information

  • Verify your medical cannabis card status
  • Track allotment usage and remaining limits
  • Facilitate purchases at licensed dispensaries
  • Provide customer support and technical assistance
  • Comply with state reporting requirements
  • Improve our services and user experience
  • Send important notifications and updates

Information Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • State Agencies: As required by South Dakota medical cannabis regulations
  • Licensed Dispensaries: For verification and transaction processing
  • Healthcare Providers: With your consent for treatment coordination
  • Service Providers: Third parties who assist in operating our platform
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or court order

Data Retention

We retain your information for the following periods:

  • Active Accounts: While your account remains active
  • Transaction Records: 7 years as required by state regulations
  • Medical Records: As required by HIPAA and state law
  • Technical Logs: 90 days for security and troubleshooting

Patient Rights & Responsibilities

Your Rights as a Patient

  • Right to Privacy: Your medical information is confidential and protected
  • Right to Access: You can view and obtain copies of your medical records
  • Right to Correction: You can request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Right to Restriction: You can request limits on how your information is used
  • Right to Portability: You can transfer your data to another provider
  • Right to Deletion: You can request deletion of your account and data
  • Right to Notification: You will be notified of any data breaches

Patient Responsibilities

  • Provide accurate and complete information
  • Keep your medical cannabis card current and valid
  • Comply with state possession and usage limits
  • Report any changes to your medical condition
  • Notify us of any security concerns or breaches
  • Use the service only for legal medical purposes
  • Maintain the confidentiality of your account credentials

South Dakota Medical Cannabis Laws

Age Restrictions:

  • 18-20 years: Limited to edibles and non-inhalant products only
  • 21+ years: Full access to all cannabis products
  • Minors: Require parental/guardian consent and supervision

Possession Limits:

  • Maximum 3 ounces of cannabis flower per month
  • Equivalent amounts for concentrates and edibles
  • Home cultivation: Up to 4 plants (where permitted)

Usage Restrictions:

  • No consumption in public places
  • No driving under the influence
  • No use on federal property
  • Respect private property rules
