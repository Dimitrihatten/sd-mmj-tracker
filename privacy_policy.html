1. Introduction & Overview

SDMMJTracker ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal health information (PHI) and other personal data. As a covered entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and a medical cannabis patient portal serving South Dakota residents, we handle sensitive medical and personal information with the highest level of care and compliance.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our medical cannabis patient verification and tracking services. We are required by law to maintain the privacy of your PHI and to provide you with this notice of our legal duties and privacy practices.

Our Commitment: We will never sell, rent, or lease your personal health information to third parties for marketing purposes. Your medical cannabis usage, health conditions, and personal data remain confidential and are only used to provide you with our services and comply with applicable laws.

5. HIPAA Protected Health Information

HIPAA Covered Entity Status

SDMMJTracker operates as a HIPAA-covered entity, which means we are legally required to protect your Protected Health Information (PHI) according to federal privacy and security standards.

Administrative Safeguards

  • Designated HIPAA Security Officer
  • Workforce training programs
  • Access management controls
  • Regular risk assessments
  • Incident response procedures
  • Business Associate Agreements

Physical Safeguards

  • Secure data centers with 24/7 monitoring
  • Biometric access controls
  • Environmental monitoring systems
  • Secure workstation policies
  • Media disposal procedures
  • Disaster recovery capabilities

Technical Safeguards

  • AES-256 encryption at rest
  • TLS 1.3 encryption in transit
  • Multi-factor authentication
  • Automated audit logging
  • Regular vulnerability scanning
  • Secure session management

Minimum Necessary Standard

We adhere to the HIPAA "minimum necessary" standard, which means we only use, disclose, or request the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose. Our staff receives regular training on this principle, and our systems are designed to limit access to PHI based on job roles and responsibilities.

7. Your Privacy Rights

HIPAA Patient Rights

Under HIPAA, you have specific rights regarding your Protected Health Information. These rights are fundamental and cannot be waived.

Right to Access

You have the right to inspect and obtain copies of your PHI maintained by us.

  • Request access within 30 days
  • Receive copies in your preferred format
  • Reasonable fees may apply for copying

Right to Amendment

You can request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete PHI.

  • Submit written requests with reasoning
  • We will respond within 60 days
  • Appeal process available if denied

Right to Restrictions

You can request limits on how we use or disclose your PHI.

  • Request specific use limitations
  • Limit disclosures to certain individuals
  • We will consider all reasonable requests

Right to Confidential Communications

You can request communications through alternative means or locations.

  • Alternative contact methods
  • Different mailing addresses
  • Secure communication preferences

Right to Accounting

You can receive a list of certain disclosures of your PHI.

  • Six-year disclosure history
  • Excludes treatment and payment disclosures
  • First request free annually

Right to File Complaints

You can file complaints about our privacy practices without retaliation.

  • File with us or HHS Office for Civil Rights
  • No retaliation for complaints
  • Written or verbal complaints accepted

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Privacy Officer using the information provided in Section 12. Most requests should be made in writing and include:

  • Your full name and contact information
  • Medical card number for verification
  • Specific right you wish to exercise
  • Detailed description of your request

10. Children's Privacy

Age Requirements for Medical Cannabis

South Dakota medical cannabis laws have specific age requirements that affect how we handle information for patients under 21.

Patients Ages 18-20

  • Limited Product Access

    Restricted to edibles and non-inhalant products only

  • Enhanced Privacy Protection

    Additional safeguards for personal information

  • Parental Involvement

    May require caregiver authorization in some cases

Patients Under 18

  • Caregiver Required

    Must have registered caregiver for all transactions

  • Restricted Data Collection

    Minimal data collection with parental consent

  • COPPA Compliance

    Additional protections under children's privacy laws

Special Protections for Minors

For patients under 21, we implement additional privacy safeguards including:

  • Enhanced encryption for all personal data
  • Restricted access to information by staff
  • Additional consent requirements for data sharing
  • Shorter data retention periods where possible
  • Enhanced notification procedures for breaches
  • Priority customer support for privacy concerns
