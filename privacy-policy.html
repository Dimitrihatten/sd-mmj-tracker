Privacy Policy

Effective Date: November 1, 2024
Last Updated: November 1, 2024

1. Information We Collect

SDMMJTracker collects and processes the following types of information to provide medical cannabis tracking services:

1.1 Personal Health Information (PHI)

  • Medical cannabis card numbers and registration information
  • Physician recommendations and medical conditions
  • Cannabis purchase history and allotment usage
  • Treatment outcomes and effectiveness data

1.2 Personal Information

  • Full name, date of birth, and contact information
  • Mailing address and ZIP code
  • Email address and phone number
  • Emergency contact information

1.3 Usage Data

  • Cannabis purchase history and dispensary visits
  • Platform usage analytics and preferences
  • Device information and IP addresses
  • Browser type and operating system

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

  • Patient Verification: Verify medical cannabis patient status with South Dakota Department of Health
  • Allotment Tracking: Monitor monthly allotment usage and ensure compliance
  • Service Delivery: Provide dispensary recommendations and price comparisons
  • Compliance Reporting: Generate required reports for regulatory authorities
  • Platform Improvement: Enhance our services and user experience
  • Communication: Send important updates and compliance notifications

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

3.1 With Your Consent

We will obtain your explicit consent before sharing any PHI with third parties for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

3.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or regulatory authorities, including:

  • South Dakota Department of Health for compliance verification
  • Law enforcement agencies with valid warrants
  • Healthcare providers for emergency medical treatment

3.3 Business Partners

We may share limited information with verified dispensary partners for:

  • Patient verification and allotment checking
  • Transaction processing and compliance reporting
  • Inventory management and product recommendations

4. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

4.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for all PHI storage
  • Transmission Security: TLS 1.3 encryption for data transmission
  • Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication for all accounts
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive logging of all data access

4.2 Physical Safeguards

  • Secure data centers with 24/7 monitoring
  • Biometric and multi-factor access controls
  • Environmental monitoring and protection
  • Secure workstation and device controls

4.3 Administrative Safeguards

  • Designated HIPAA Security Officer
  • Regular workforce training and access management
  • Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with vendors
  • Regular security risk assessments and audits

5. Your Privacy Rights

Under HIPAA and applicable privacy laws, you have the following rights:

5.1 Access Rights

  • Right to access and obtain copies of your PHI
  • Right to request your information in electronic format
  • Right to direct copies to third parties

5.2 Amendment Rights

  • Right to request amendments to your PHI
  • Right to have incorrect information corrected
  • Right to add supplemental information

5.3 Restriction Rights

  • Right to request restrictions on use and disclosure
  • Right to request confidential communications
  • Right to object to certain disclosures

5.4 Complaint Rights

  • Right to file complaints about our privacy practices
  • Right to file complaints with the Department of Health and Human Services
  • No retaliation for filing complaints

6. Data Retention

We retain your information for the following periods:

  • Active Patient Data: As long as your medical card is valid plus 6 years
  • Purchase History: 7 years from last transaction for compliance purposes
  • Audit Logs: 6 years for HIPAA compliance requirements
  • Marketing Data: Until you opt-out or close your account

7. International Data Transfers

Your data is processed and stored within the United States. We do not transfer PHI outside the United States without explicit consent and appropriate safeguards.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18, except as permitted by South Dakota medical cannabis laws with appropriate parental consent and physician oversight.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Sending email notifications to registered users
  • Providing 30 days advance notice for significant changes

10. State-Specific Privacy Rights

South Dakota residents have additional rights under state law, including the right to know about data collection practices and request deletion of personal information not required for compliance purposes.
