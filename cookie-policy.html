Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and similar technologies

Effective Date: January 1, 2024

Last Updated: January 1, 2024

This Cookie Policy explains how SD MMJ Tracker ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website and use our services.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, such as your preferred language and other settings.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility.

  • Authentication cookies: Remember your login status
  • Security cookies: Prevent unauthorized access
  • Session cookies: Maintain your session state

Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance functionality and personalization. They remember your preferences and settings.

  • Preference cookies: Remember your language and display settings
  • Accessibility cookies: Remember accessibility preferences
  • Form data cookies: Remember partially completed forms

Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information.

  • Analytics cookies: Track page views and user interactions
  • Performance cookies: Monitor website performance
  • Error tracking cookies: Help identify and fix issues

HIPAA Compliance and Medical Data

Important: We do not store any protected health information (PHI) in cookies. All medical information is handled in compliance with HIPAA regulations and stored securely on our servers.

Cookies used on our site may contain:

  • Session identifiers (encrypted and anonymized)
  • User preferences and settings
  • Authentication tokens (encrypted)
  • Technical information for functionality

Cookies do NOT contain:

  • Medical card numbers
  • Health information
  • Personal identification information
  • Medical cannabis usage data

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies. These include:

Google Fonts

We use Google Fonts to display fonts on our website. Google may set cookies for this service.

Content Delivery Networks (CDN)

We may use CDN services to improve website performance. These services may set performance-related cookies.

Security Services

We use security services to protect against attacks. These may set security-related cookies.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete specific cookies or all cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies

Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly:

  • You may need to log in repeatedly
  • Your preferences may not be saved
  • Some functionality may be limited
  • Patient verification may not work correctly

Data Retention

Different types of cookies are retained for different periods:

Session Cookies

Deleted when you close your browser

Persistent Cookies

  • Authentication cookies: 30 days
  • Preference cookies: 1 year
  • Analytics cookies: 2 years

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Providing notice through our service
